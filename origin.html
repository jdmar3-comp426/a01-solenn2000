Origin

The Moira, also known as the Atropos in Greek, originated as a daemon (spirit) from the pre-Greek Mycenaean religon. An important Mycenaean belief was that all of the events or actions were a part of destiny. They accepted inevitability in the natural order of things.

The Moirai appear as the personification of fate in the Odyssey, but do not yet have separate names. The Moirai are eventually elevated to godesses. In Hesiod's poem, they are portrayed as godesses who do not only punish men, but also Gods for their sins.

The Mycenaean civilization was located in Mainland Greece, the Aegan islands and Western Anatolia.
