The Moirai

The Moira, also known as "the Fates" in English, embodied destiny in Greek Mythology. These three sisters were the daughters of Zeus. Their mother has changed over time varying from being Nxy ("night") and Titaness Themis, the embodiment of divine law. They had much power in ensuring that mortals lived their lives according to the fate that was assigned to them by the laws of the universe.

Parallels

In Roman mythology,three women known as the Parcae or Fata recite oracle, declarations and know of mortals' destiny. Likewise, in Norse mythology, there ia a trio of women called the Norns who rule the destiny of both men and gods.
