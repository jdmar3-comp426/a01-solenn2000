Clotho

Clotho is the Moirai who spins the thread of human life. She decides when a person is born or when they are killed. Along with Hermes, she was given credit for creating the Alphabet of her people.

Lachesis

Lachesis, normally clothed in white and shown with a rod in hand, is the aportioner wo measures the thread of life spun by Clotho. She determines how much time for life is allowed for each person, deciding the person's destiny after the thread is spun. She is known to appear three days after a baby is born to decide its fate.

Atropos

Atropos is the oldest of the Three Fates and was known as "the Inflexible One". She is the one who ends the life of mortals by cutting the thread and chooses the manner in which they die. She appears in multiple stories such as Achilles.
